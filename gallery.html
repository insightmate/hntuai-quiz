The sixteen archetypes

You do not choose your archetype. You are already living as one of them.

Everyone meets the machine through the same eight tensions. Each one has two faces — one that quietly closes you down, one that opens you up. Here are all sixteen. The diagnostic is what tells you which face is yours, and it does not flatter.

Vision

How you hold a future too large to hold

Where you hide

The Fatalist

Decides how it ends — doom or rapture — and takes their hands off the wheel. They call the surrender realism.

Where you lead

The Diviner

Reads the future like weather and moves inside it. Sonar, not prophecy; the hands stay on.

Value

What your time is actually for

Where you hide

The Hustler

Time is precious, so never waste a second — every hour has to show something for it. Somehow the saved time keeps vanishing.

Where you lead

The Humanist

Time is precious, so they refuse to spend it all producing. They decide what it is for, and they live it.

Voice

The risk of being seen in your own work

Where you hide

The Automaton

Feels exposure precisely, and sands it off before anyone else can find it. Hidden, not empty.

Where you lead

The Auteur

Feels presence precisely, and keeps theirs in on purpose. They refused to disappear.

Agency

Whether the body gets a vote

Where you hide

The Captive

The body says this is wrong — and they swallow it, and explain why the cost is acceptable.

Where you lead

The Cultivator

The body says this is wrong — and they believe it, and walk toward what it recognises as real.

Boundaries

Taste as a door, or taste as a fence

Where you hide

The Purist

Protects the real by inspecting everyone else's work for tells, and redrawing the line each time they move.

Where you lead

The Trickster

Protects the real by owning the conditions they work under, so the question is settled at the source.

Partnership

Who gets to author your belonging

Where you hide

The Companion Seeker

Meets the longing with something that can never reject them — so it can never hurt them, and never quite reach them.

Where you lead

The Kin Builder

Meets the longing with people, and with a tool that can disagree — so what witnesses them can actually see them.

Flow

Control, or emergence

Where you hide

The Controller

Believes mediocrity comes from too little control, so they specify everything and fight every deviation out.

Where you lead

The Creatrix

Believes mediocrity comes from too much control, so they convene the conditions and author the work by choosing what to keep.

Changemaking

Naming what is wrong, or building what is next

Where you hide

The Reformer

Names exactly what is wrong, in devastating detail, to the people who will not change it. Being right is not the same as being effective.

Where you lead

The Worldbuilder

Takes the same map and builds the alternative — the house people can actually leave the old one for.

And past the sixteen, for the one in a hundred, there is a seventeenth. It is not on this page. You cannot fake your way into it.

The only way to know which one is you is to answer for the person you actually are — not the one you would like to be.
