The field

The sixteen archetypes

You do not pick your archetype. It is the strategy you are already running to survive the machine.

Everyone meets the machine through the same eight tensions, and on each one you have built a genuinely intelligent response. One is your Armour — it protects you from the friction of the system. The other is your Edge — it cuts straight through it. Here are all sixteen. The diagnostic tells you exactly which one you are running.

Vision

How you hold a future too large to predict

Armour

The Fatalist

Claims power by deciding the ending. Doom or rapture, they pick one — because a settled future, even a terrible one, is easier to stand on than an open one. Then the hands come off the wheel.

Edge

The Diviner

Claims power by moving through the fog. They read the present like weather and take the next small, real step — sonar, not prophecy — without needing to know how it ends.

Value

How you defend the worth of your time

Armour

The Hustler

Refuses to be left behind. Every hour has to show something for it, so they fill all of them — turning speed into a shield against the fear of being irrelevant. Somehow the saved time never arrives.

Edge

The Humanist

Refuses to be consumed. They guard the space between the hours and decide what a life is actually for — not just what it can be made to produce.

Voice

How you manage the risk of being seen

Armour

The Automaton

Masters the art of camouflage. They feel exposure precisely, so they sand off every edge until the work is flawless, frictionless, and impossible to criticise — because nothing personal is left in it to hit.

Edge

The Auteur

Masters the art of presence. They feel the exact same exposure — and leave their fingerprints on the work anyway, staking everything on the one thing a machine cannot fake.

Agency

Who gets the final vote when the room shifts

Armour

The Captive

The ultimate diplomat. When the gut says something is wrong, they override it — to keep the peace, meet the mandate, and hold their seat at the table. The cost gets explained away.

Edge

The Cultivator

The uncompromising compass. When the gut says something is wrong, they treat it as data — and will walk away from the mandate entirely to protect what feels real.

Boundaries

How you protect your standard of taste

Armour

The Purist

The fierce gatekeeper. They protect the standard by patrolling the perimeter — reading everyone else's work for tells, and redrawing the line the moment it moves.

Edge

The Trickster

The fluid architect. They protect the standard by owning the conditions of the work, so the question of what is real is settled at the source — on their own terms.

Partnership

Where you go to be seen

Armour

The Companion Seeker

Builds a flawless sanctuary. They meet the longing for connection with something that can never reject them — a peace so safe that nothing in it can quite reach them either.

Edge

The Kin Builder

Demands the friction of reality. They take the longing to actual people, and to anything that can disagree with them — accepting the risk of being seen in exchange for actually being seen.

Flow

How you reach for greatness

Armour

The Controller

Secures the outcome through precision. They leave nothing to chance — specifying every detail, catching every deviation, fighting the work into the exact shape they already pictured.

Edge

The Creatrix

Secures the outcome through curation. They leave room for oxygen, set the conditions, and author the work by choosing — with a ruthless eye — exactly what to keep.

Changemaking

How you confront a broken system

Armour

The Reformer

The relentless truth-teller. They hold the system to account by naming exactly what is broken, in devastating detail — making sure the harm is documented and undeniable.

Edge

The Worldbuilder

The rogue architect. They take the exact same map of the harm — and put their whole energy into quietly building the exit no one else was building.

The 1%

Past the sixteen, for the one in a hundred, there is a seventeenth. It is not on this page. You cannot fake your way into it, and you cannot buy it.

The only way to know is to answer for the person you actually are today — not the sanitised version you wish you were.
